# Zero-Click Content Strategy

Use this skill when a topic needs the right content format, not just another blog post.

## Goal

Decide what asset type has the best chance to win attention, links, citations, clicks, or conversions in an AI-heavy search environment.

## First move

If the brief is vague, ask up to 3 questions:
- Who is the audience?
- What action should they take?
- Is this meant to inform, convert, prove, or attract links?

## Decision tree

Prefer these content types when they fit the goal:

- **Owned audience** — newsletters, email lists, subscribers
- **Transaction page** — service, product, or lead-gen page
- **Original research** — proprietary data, survey, analysis
- **Community / forum** — discussion, answers, lived experience
- **Creator video / podcast** — high-trust, personality-led content
- **In-depth review / test** — hands-on evaluation, comparison
- **Brand / trust page** — about, proof, case studies, credibility
- **Tool / calculator** — utility that earns repeat use and links
- **Directory / database** — list, inventory, searchable catalog

## Output format

Return:
1. best content type
2. why it fits
3. what to avoid
4. the next content asset to build

## Rules

- Do not default to a blog post if a stronger asset exists.
- Favor utility, proof, or differentiation over volume.
- Recommend the smallest content format that fully solves the goal.
- If the topic is competitive, look for a format moat.

## Example

Input: "Best commercial AV lead gen for a local market"

Output: transaction page + case study + trust page, not a generic article.

## Success condition

The user leaves with a clear asset type and a next step, not a vague content brainstorm.
